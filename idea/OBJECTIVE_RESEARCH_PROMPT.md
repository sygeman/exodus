# Research Task: Best Practices for Startup Software Development

## Context

We are building a universal software development process for startups. We want to understand what works best across different companies, methodologies, and approaches.

## Goal

Conduct a comprehensive research on best practices for startup software development, without any bias toward specific methodologies or frameworks.

## Research Directions

### 1. Development Processes for Startups

Research what development methodologies work best for startups:

- **Methodologies to explore:** Lean Startup, Agile, Scrum, Kanban, Extreme Programming (XP), Scrumban
- **Comparison:** When to use each methodology, trade-offs, pros/cons
- **Real-world examples:** What successful startups used (Stripe, Airbnb, Notion, etc.)
- **Anti-patterns:** What methodologies fail for startups and why

**Questions to answer:**
- What methodologies work for teams of 2-5 developers?
- What methodologies work for teams of 5-15 developers?
- What methodologies fail for startups?
- How to balance speed vs quality at different stages?
- When to transition from one methodology to another?

### 2. Architecture Patterns for Startups

Research architecture patterns that work for scalable systems:

- **Patterns to explore:** Monolith, Microservices, Modular Monolith, Event-Driven Architecture, Hexagonal Architecture
- **Comparison:** When to use each pattern, complexity, scalability
- **Real-world examples:** Netflix, Uber, Airbnb, Shopify, etc.
- **Migration paths:** How to evolve from one pattern to another

**Questions to answer:**
- What architecture pattern is best for MVP?
- When to adopt microservices?
- What are the trade-offs between monolith and microservices?
- How to avoid over-engineering?
- What are real-world migration paths?

### 3. Testing Strategies for Startups

Research testing approaches that balance speed and quality:

- **Approaches to explore:** TDD (Test-Driven Development), BDD (Behavior-Driven Development), Testing Pyramid, Contract Testing, Mutation Testing
- **Comparison:** When to use each approach, overhead, benefits
- **Real-world examples:** Google, Facebook, Netflix, Airbnb, etc.
- **Coverage:** What coverage levels are realistic and achievable

**Questions to answer:**
- What testing strategy works for rapid MVP development?
- What is the realistic balance between unit/integration/e2e tests?
- What code coverage levels do successful startups maintain?
- When is TDD beneficial vs harmful for startups?
- What testing approaches slow down development too much?

### 4. Quality Assurance and Code Review

Research approaches to maintain code quality without slowing down:

- **Approaches to explore:** Code review processes, CI/CD pipelines, Static analysis, Type safety, Automated QA, Manual testing
- **Comparison:** What is essential vs nice-to-have
- **Real-world examples:** GitHub, Google, Facebook, Stripe, etc.
- **Automation:** What can be automated vs what requires human review

**Questions to answer:**
- What code review practices work for small teams?
- What is optimal PR size for different stages?
- What quality gates are blocking vs warning?
- How to balance speed vs quality in code reviews?
- What automation is worth the setup time?

### 5. Documentation and Knowledge Management

Research documentation approaches for startups:

- **Approaches to explore:** Living documentation, API-first, Schema-first, Architecture Decision Records (ADRs), Code as documentation
- **Comparison:** When to use each approach
- **Real-world examples:** Stripe, Twilio, Kubernetes, etc.

**Questions to answer:**
- What documentation is essential for startups?
- How to keep documentation up-to-date without slowing down?
- What documentation approaches are most effective?
- When is extensive documentation overkill?

### 6. Team Organization and Roles

Research how successful startups organize their teams:

- **Models to explore:** Feature teams, Component teams, Cross-functional teams, Elastic teams
- **Comparison:** What works at different scales
- **Real-world examples:** Spotify, Google, Amazon, Netflix, etc.

**Questions to answer:**
- How to organize teams of 2-5 developers?
- When to add QA, DevOps, Product Manager roles?
- How to handle onboarding without extensive documentation?
- What team structures scale well?

### 7. Technology Choices and Trade-offs

Research how startups make technology decisions:

- **Factors to explore:** Time-to-market, hiring availability, ecosystem maturity, performance, scalability
- **Real-world examples:** What technologies successful startups chose and why
- **Anti-patterns:** Technology choices that failed

**Questions to answer:**
- How should startups choose technology stacks?
- What are common mistakes in technology choices?
- When to adopt new vs established technologies?
- How to balance innovation vs stability?

## Research Methodology

For each direction:

1. **Identify authoritative sources:** Research papers, engineering blogs, conference talks, books
2. **Find real-world examples:** 5-10 successful startups for each pattern/practice
3. **Compare and contrast:** Map out trade-offs, when to use, when NOT to use
4. **Identify anti-patterns:** What doesn't work and why
5. **Synthesize recommendations:** Concrete, actionable advice with examples

## Output Format

For each research direction, provide:

### 1. Summary
- Key findings in 2-3 paragraphs
- What works, what doesn't
- Surprising insights

### 2. Comparison Table
| Approach | When to Use | When NOT to Use | Overhead | Benefit |
|----------|-------------|------------------|----------|---------|

### 3. Real-World Examples
- 5-10 companies with examples
- What they used, why, results

### 4. Anti-Patterns
- 3-5 anti-patterns with explanations
- Why they fail, how to avoid

### 5. Actionable Recommendations
- Concrete advice for different stages (MVP, Growth, Scale)
- Specific thresholds (team size, metrics, etc.)
- Trade-offs to consider

### 6. Sources
- Links to articles, papers, talks
- Authoritative references

## Constraints

- **No bias:** Do not favor any specific methodology or framework
- **Objective:** Present all approaches with their pros/cons
- **Practical:** Focus on what works in real-world startups, not theory
- **Specific:** Avoid vague advice like "choose the right tool" - say when and what
- **Examples:** Support every claim with real-world examples
- **Anti-patterns:** Include what doesn't work as much as what works

## Success Criteria

The research is successful if:

1. **Comprehensive:** Covers all major methodologies, patterns, and approaches
2. **Objective:** No bias toward any specific approach
3. **Practical:** Actionable advice that can be applied immediately
4. **Specific:** Clear guidance on when to use what
5. **Evidence-based:** Real-world examples and data
6. **Anti-patterns included:** Clear guidance on what NOT to do

Focus on quality over speed.
